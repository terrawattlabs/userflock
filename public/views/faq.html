Fundamentals

What is Customer Discovery Ninja?

Customer Discovery Ninja is a lean startup tool built to help validate a new idea, business, product, or service by conducting first-hand market research with US consumers. Instead of figuring out and worrying about who to talk to and how to reach them, Customer Discovery Ninja has people call you. This provides both convenience and unbiased feedback.

What is Customer Development / Customer Discovery?

Customer Development is a four-step framework developed by serial entrepreneur and business school Professor Steve Blank for discovering and validating the right market for your idea, building the right product features that solve customers’ needs, testing the correct model for acquiring and converting customers, and deploying the right organization and resources to scale the business.

Customer Discovery is the first stage in this process. It’s all about “getting out of the building” to learn more about your potential customers and their problems, not pitching your solution.

FAQ

Who will be calling me?

US consumers that, on average, are younger and more educated that the general population. Your results will vary but every caller will be from the US. You can also filter calls by requesting people that “live in a southern state” or “know a direct relative with ADHD”.

How many calls can I expect to receive?

This answer will vary depending on the time of day, how narrow your target customer is, timing, and a few other factors, but in general, you should expect to conduct 4, ten-minute interviews in an hour. If you’re lucky it could be as much as 6 in an hour; it could also be as low as 1 or 2.

I submitted my interview request, when will people start calling?

There is usually a 5-10 minute delay before you receive your first call but it could be a little longer if your target customer is narrowly defined.

Why do people take time to call in and talk to me?

Callers are paid a small reward for their time and effort for calling in and speaking with you. Most callers do this because they are killing time – it’s a substitute for solitaire, not a job.

How do I know people won’t just call in and not talk or give good answers?

You provide a simple reward code to them at the completion of your interview that confirms they answered your questions appropriately.

Doesn’t paying them mean there is a natural bias?

There is a slight bias known as the ‘Selection bias’ related to individuals opting-in to a study that is especially true when participants are paid. However, Customer Discovery Ninja is designed to be used in the early stages of an idea or venture to find out more about peoples’ problems in general. The callers are much less biased and impartial than friends, family, and other colleagues since they don’t know you. Additionally, customer discovery is a process that takes dozens of interviews to get proper data and recognize trends.

What if someone likes my idea and wants to steal it?

First of all, you shouldn’t be talking about your idea during these interviews. Second, the probability of someone hearing a sentence or two about your idea, stealing it, and executing it to completion is very low.

Best Practices

How can I get the most out of these interviews?

Proper interview techniques are an art, not a science. The best way to get better at them is to practice. You could also read up on ethnography and proper interview techniques online. I thought customers don’t know what they want?

That’s true – customers won’t tell you what solution to build, that’s your job. But they will tell you their problems worth solving.

Henry Ford once said, “If I had asked people what they wanted, they would have said faster horses.” Most people think this quote means talking to customers is pointless but it still reveals a problem they were looking to solve – getting from point A to point B faster. Your job is to build the solution that solves this.

Why should a founder waste their time talking to customers?

We love founders; they’re a special breed that wants to change the world and will take a risk to do so. Since they’re the visionaries behind a new idea they need to conduct the market research first-hand, not outsource it to someone else. Customer discovery is testing your riskiest assumptions, many of which are wrong. When someone else reports back bad news they get fired, when a founder does, the team pivots.

Why don’t I just read all of the latest market reports instead of talking to customers?

Market reports are great for finding large trends to leverage but are not a replacement for in-depth interviews with real people.

Why don’t I just use a survey or focus group?

Surveys and focus groups are useful for fine-tuning a product or validating learnings at scale but again, they are not a replacement for first-hand conversations.

I’ve interviewed dozens of customers with your tool and learned a lot, now what?

Apply all of your learning to the next step. Set up a landing page to test your solution and see if people will start giving you some type of currency (an email address, money, referral, etc.). With all of your customer discovery interviews, you’ll know exactly what to say and have the best marketing copy of all – the words of your customer.

How do I turn these customer interviews into a positive for investors and raising money?

There’s nothing better than customer quotes and first-hand market research that shows your company is less risky to invest in. The best vision can easily be a hallucination without testing it in the real world.
